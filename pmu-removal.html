What You Need to Know

Designed for PMU Work

This service is intended exclusively for permanent makeup and cosmetic tattoo pigments. Saline-based removal works by drawing pigment up through the skin via osmosis, allowing it to shed naturally as the skin heals. Because it does not rely on heat or laser energy, it is suitable for a wider range of pigment colours than laser-based methods.

Cosmetic tattoo pigments in warm tones including brown, taupe, and soft black generally respond well to this method. Pigments that are very dark, heavily saturated, or contain metallic compounds including black, grey, silver, green, or blue tones require individual assessment, as outcomes in these cases are less predictable and cannot be guaranteed. We will always provide an honest evaluation at consultation before any treatment proceeds.

Results are gradual and cumulative. Individual outcomes depend on pigment depth, skin type, aftercare compliance, and how the body responds to treatment. We do not make outcome guarantees and will always tell you honestly what we believe is achievable for your specific situation.

Please note: This service is for cosmetic tattoo pigments only. Results vary by individual and cannot be guaranteed. A consultation is required before any treatment is performed and we reserve the right to decline treatment where suitability is uncertain.

The Process

How It Works

1

Consultation

We assess the pigment type, colour, depth, and skin condition to determine suitability and discuss realistic expectations before any treatment is scheduled.

2

Treatment

A saline-based solution is applied to the treated area using a precise method designed to reach the pigment layer and initiate the osmosis process.

3

Healing

The skin forms a protective scab over 7 to 14 days. As it heals and sheds naturally, some pigment may be expelled in this process. The degree of change varies by individual skin response and pigment characteristics.

4

Review

Once fully healed, we assess progress together and determine the appropriate next step, whether that is a further session, a correction, or no additional treatment.
