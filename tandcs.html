Informed Consent

Cosmetic tattooing (including microblading, nano brows, ombre brows, eyeliner, lip blush, beauty marks, freckles and related procedures) is a form of semi-permanent makeup that involves depositing pigment into the skin. By proceeding with a booking you acknowledge and accept the following:

  • The procedure involves the use of needles and pigments applied to the skin, which may cause mild discomfort, redness, swelling and tenderness in the treated area
  • Results are semi-permanent and will fade over time. Colour, shape and retention will vary between individuals depending on skin type, lifestyle, sun exposure, skincare products and aftercare
  • Immediately after the procedure the colour will appear darker and bolder than the final result. The true colour and shape will settle over the following weeks as the skin goes through its natural recovery process
  • Multiple sessions are typically required to achieve the desired result. The initial session creates the foundation and a follow-up session is recommended to refine the shape, density and colour
  • Jessica will design and customise your treatment based on your facial features, preferences and skin tone. While every effort is made to achieve symmetry and your desired outcome, perfect symmetry is not naturally occurring and minor variations are normal
  • You have had the opportunity to ask questions about the procedure, aftercare, expected results and potential risks before proceeding

Who We Cannot Treat

For your safety, we are unable to perform cosmetic tattoo procedures on the following individuals:

  • Those under 16 years of age
  • Those under 18 years of age unless accompanied by a parent or legal guardian who provides written consent on the day of the appointment
  • People with diabetes unless you have written clearance from your doctor
  • Pregnant or lactating women
  • People with glaucoma or those taking blood thinning medications (e.g. aspirin, warfarin)
  • People with any active skin disease, irritation or infection in or around the treatment area
  • People with known allergies to makeup, pigments or colours
  • Those with easily triggered post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
  • Those with a history of keloid or hypertrophic scarring
  • Those with active skin cancer in the area to be treated
  • Those with haemophilia or a bleeding disorder
  • Those with healing disorders, uncontrolled high blood pressure, heart conditions or mitral valve disorder
  • Those on skin medications such as Roaccutane (must wait 6 to 12 months after completing the course) or steroids that thin and sensitise the skin
  • Those with serious medical conditions such as cancer, epilepsy, seizures or autoimmune disorders
  • Those currently undergoing radiotherapy or chemotherapy
  • Those with transmittable blood-borne conditions such as HIV or Hepatitis
  • Those who have recently had Botox, Dysport or dermal filler in or near the treatment area (must wait at least 6 weeks)

Please let us know if you have or have had allergic reactions to topical anaesthetics. We use lidocaine and prilocaine.

If you are under the care of a physician you must obtain written clearance from them prior to your procedure.

Failure to disclose relevant information may compromise the safety and results of the procedure. Allure Microblading accepts no liability for complications, adverse reactions or unsatisfactory results arising from incomplete or inaccurate information provided by the client.

By proceeding with a booking you confirm that the information you have provided is true and complete to the best of your knowledge.

Patch Testing

A patch test is available upon request at additional cost and must be completed at least 48 hours before the main procedure. If you choose not to undertake a patch test, you accept the risk and release the technician from any liability related to allergic reactions or sensitivities to the pigments, topical anaesthetics or other products used during treatment.

Please be aware that there are traces of Nickel in the needles and pigments used. The patch test may not conclusively confirm a Nickel allergy or predict all possible reactions to the products used.

Results and Limitations

Every effort is made to deliver the best possible outcome for each client. However, Allure Microblading cannot guarantee specific results as each person's skin responds to pigment and technique differently.

Factors that influence results include skin type, skin condition, oiliness, health conditions, medications, lifestyle, sun exposure, skincare routine and how closely aftercare instructions are followed. Clients with oily skin, large pores or certain skin conditions may experience faster fading, less defined strokes or lower pigment retention.

If you are unhappy with the outcome of your treatment, please contact us. We are committed to working with you and will always do our best to achieve a result you are happy with. However, we do not offer refunds or free corrections as the treatment has been carried out at our time and cost, using single-use materials.

Aftercare

Detailed aftercare instructions will be provided following your procedure. It is your responsibility to follow these instructions carefully, as proper aftercare is essential to achieving the best possible result and avoiding complications.

Allure Microblading accepts no responsibility for poor results, pigment loss, infection or other complications that arise from failure to follow the aftercare instructions provided.
